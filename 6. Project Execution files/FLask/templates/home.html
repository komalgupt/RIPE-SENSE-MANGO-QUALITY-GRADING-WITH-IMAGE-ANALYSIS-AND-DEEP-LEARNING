Why Ripeness Matters for Mangoes

  • Ripening has transformed the world of bananas, avocados, pears, kiwifruit, and other agricultural items.
  • Ripening techniques (also known as pre-conditioning) have improved consumer satisfaction and sales of ripened fruit.
  • Identifying the ripening stage of mango is critical because it affects the fruit's quality and flavor.
  • Mangoes are harvested when they are mature but still firm, after which they ripen on the tree.
  • Mango ripening can be divided into three stages: underripe, perfectly ripe, and overripe.
  • Mangoes must be harvested at the appropriate ripeness stage to acquire the best flavor, texture, and color. Mangoes harvested too early will fail to ripen correctly, resulting in a dull taste and fibrous texture.

The Stages of Ripening of Mango

The ripening process of mangoes involves a series of changes in their color, texture, and flavor. The stages of ripening of mango are as follows:

  1. Green Stage: The mango is firm and unripe, with a green color and no aroma.
  2. Maturing Stage: The mango starts to mature and turns from green to yellow. It has a slightly sweet aroma and is still firm to the touch.
  3. Ripening Stage: The mango is fully ripe and has turned from yellow to orange or red, depending on the variety. It has a sweet aroma, is slightly soft to the touch, and has a juicy flesh.
  4. Overripe Stage: The mango has become overripe and is soft to the touch. It may have wrinkles on its skin, and the flesh may be mushy and sour.
